About

Engineer by training, neuroscientist by calling

I am an electrical engineer and neuroscientist with a rare combination of backgrounds: a foundation in information theory, signal processing, and network modelling from my engineering degrees in Brazil, and a PhD in neuroscience from Newcastle University funded by a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellowship (1 position from ~100 applicants).

My PhD work focused on the motor system of awake, behaving non-human primates. I was among the first researchers to simultaneously record high-density neural activity from primary motor cortex (M1) and the brainstem reticular formation (RF) — showing that information flow between these structures is bidirectional, not simply top-down, and that rotational dynamics previously associated with cortex also exist in subcortical motor regions.

Alongside academia, I have worked as a product manager at AI startups and as a new product development manager building FPGA-based hardware systems. I can design an experiment, build the data pipeline to analyse it, and ship a product — all three.

I am fluent in Portuguese, English, and Italian, and have collaborated at Aix-Marseille University and FORTH Institute in Crete.

Research

What I work on

My work sits at the intersection of systems neuroscience, dynamical systems theory, and information theory — with a particular focus on how distributed brain circuits coordinate to produce movement.

🧠

Cortical–subcortical motor dynamics

Simultaneous high-density recordings from primary motor cortex and the brainstem reticular formation in behaving primates. Demonstrating bidirectional information flow and shared rotational dynamics across hierarchical motor structures.

📡

Information-theoretic analysis of neural data

Applying transfer entropy, interaction information, and directed connectivity measures to high-dimensional spike train data. Understanding what information flows where, and when, during voluntary movement.

🌀

Dynamical systems & population geometry

Using jPCA, dimensionality reduction, and latent dynamical models to reveal low-dimensional structure in neural population activity. Investigating how population trajectories encode movement across brain regions.

⚡

Neural data infrastructure

Designing and implementing big data pipelines for multi-area, high-density electrophysiology. Experience with Neuropixels, custom recording setups, and reproducible open-science workflows.

🔗

Network modelling in neurological disorders

Prior work characterising functional and effective connectivity during absence seizures in epilepsy using EEG, in partnership with Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein in Brazil.

🔧

Hardware & embedded systems for neuroscience

FPGA programming, embedded systems design, and IoT security — bridging the gap between neural recording hardware and the algorithms that interpret it.
