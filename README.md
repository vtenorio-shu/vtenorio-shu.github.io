## Research

My work sits at the intersection of systems neuroscience, dynamical systems theory, and information theory.
I study how the brain controls movement — from motor cortex all the way down to the brainstem — and I am particularly interested in the distributed, bidirectional nature of that process.

My PhD at Newcastle University (Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow, IN2PrimateBrains consortium) involved simultaneous high-density electrophysiology across cortical and subcortical motor structures in behaving primates, combined with information-theoretic and population-level analyses.
